Failure Analysis

Understanding capacitor failures and prevention methods

Common Failure Modes

Electrolytic Capacitors

  • Dry-out: Evaporation of electrolyte leading to increased ESR and capacitance drop
  • Pressure Relief: Venting due to gas generation from electrolyte decomposition
  • Electrical Short: Dielectric breakdown due to voltage stress or defects
  • Open Circuit: Lead connection failure or internal disconnection

Solid Polymer Capacitors

  • ESD Damage: Gate oxide damage from electrostatic discharge
  • Thermal Runaway: Overheating due to excessive ripple current
  • Dielectric Breakdown: Voltage stress exceeding rated value
  • Conductive Polymer Degradation: Long-term material degradation

Film Capacitors

  • Dielectric Breakdown: Partial discharge leading to short circuits
  • Moisture Ingress: Degradation due to environmental moisture
  • Termination Corrosion: Connection degradation over time
  • Self-Healing Stress: Repeated self-healing events reducing lifetime

Prevention Strategies

Proper Component Selection

  • Sufficient voltage derating (typically 20-50%)
  • Appropriate temperature rating for application
  • Consideration of ripple current requirements
  • Selection of appropriate technology for application

Thermal Management

  • Adequate ventilation and heat sinking
  • Consideration of ambient temperature
  • Thermal simulation during design phase
  • Proper PCB layout for heat dissipation

Environmental Protection

  • Humidity and moisture protection
  • Proper storage conditions
  • Protection from vibration and shock
  • EMI/RFI filtering to prevent electrical stress

Failure Analysis Process

When a capacitor failure occurs, our technical team follows a systematic approach to determine the root cause:

  1. Visual Inspection: Examination for physical damage, discoloration, or leakage
  2. Electrical Testing: Measurement of capacitance, ESR, leakage current, and insulation resistance
  3. Environmental Analysis: Evaluation of application conditions including temperature, voltage, and ripple current
  4. Root Cause Determination: Identification of the primary failure mechanism
  5. Recommendations: Suggestions for preventing future failures
