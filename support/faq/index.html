Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to common questions about Aishi capacitors and applications

General Product Questions

What is the warranty period for Aishi capacitors?

Aishi capacitors are guaranteed for 2 years from the date of shipment when stored and used under appropriate conditions. For automotive-grade products, the warranty extends to match automotive industry standards.

How do I decode Aishi part numbers?

Aishi part numbers follow the format: Series- Capacitance-Voltage-Packaging. For example, CD11X-1000µF-25V-RAD indicates a CD11X series capacitor with 1000µF capacitance, 25V rating, and radial lead packaging. For detailed decoding, please refer to our Product Guide or contact our support team.

Are Aishi capacitors RoHS compliant?

Yes, all Aishi capacitors are RoHS compliant and meet the requirements of the Restriction of Hazardous Substances Directive. Detailed compliance documentation is available upon request.

Technical Questions

How do I properly calculate capacitor life in my application?

Capacitor life is calculated using the formula: L = L₀ × fT × fV × fR, where L₀ is the rated life at maximum temperature and ripple current, fT is the temperature acceleration factor, fV is the voltage stress factor, and fR is the ripple current factor. Contact our FAE team for application-specific calculations.

What's the difference between radial and snap-in capacitors?

Radial capacitors have both leads on the same end (usually the bottom) and are mounted through-hole on PCBs. Snap-in capacitors have a base that fits into a mounting cup and are typically used for higher capacitance values requiring chassis mounting.

Can I parallel capacitors to increase total capacitance?

Yes, capacitors can be connected in parallel to increase total capacitance and ripple current capability. When paralleling capacitors, ensure they are of the same type, voltage rating, and ideally the same ESR for equal current sharing.

Application Questions

Which capacitor technology is best for high-frequency switching applications?

For high-frequency applications, solid polymer capacitors offer the lowest ESR and excellent high-frequency characteristics. For applications where cost is a concern, specialized low-ESR electrolytic capacitors are also suitable. Film capacitors are preferred for very high-frequency applications above several MHz.

How do I select the right capacitor for LED driver applications?

For LED drivers, consider high-temperature operation (105°C), high ripple current capability, and long operational life. Aishi's high-temperature electrolytic capacitors are specifically designed for LED applications. For output filtering, solid polymer capacitors provide excellent ripple reduction.

What derating guidelines should I follow for Aishi capacitors?

General derating guidelines: voltage derating of 20-50% depending on application, temperature derating for extended life, and ripple current derating for longer operational life. For critical applications, consult our Application Notes or contact our technical support team for specific recommendations.
