Introduction

Proper selection of Aishi capacitors is critical to the performance, reliability, and longevity of your electronic designs. This guide provides comprehensive information on selecting the right Aishi capacitor based on your application's specific requirements.

Key Parameters for Selection

Ripple Current Requirements

The effective ripple current (Ir) is one of the most critical parameters in capacitor selection. Aishi capacitors are rated with specific ripple current values at 120Hz and +20°C. For applications with higher ripple current requirements, use capacitors with higher ripple current ratings.

As a general rule, select a capacitor with a ripple current rating at least 20% higher than your calculated application requirement to ensure long-term reliability.

Capacitance Value

The required capacitance depends on your application's filtering, timing, or energy storage needs. Consider the following:

  • For power supply filtering: C = Iout / (2 × f × dV), where dV is the acceptable ripple voltage
  • For timing applications: Use the R×C time constant formula
  • For energy storage: E = ½ CV²

Rated Voltage

Select a rated voltage at least 1.3 times higher than the maximum voltage present in your application. This derating ensures reliable operation and extends operational life. Aishi capacitors are available in a wide range of voltage ratings from 4V to 500V.

Operating Temperature

Aishi capacitors are available with temperature ratings of -40°C to +85°C, -40°C to +105°C, and in some series up to +125°C. Select the appropriate temperature rating based on your application's operating environment.

Aishi Series Selection by Application

General Purpose Applications

For applications with moderate requirements, Aishi's CD11 series provides excellent general-purpose performance with standard life ratings of 2000 hours at rated temperature.

Long Life Applications

For applications requiring extended operational life, such as LED lighting or solar inverters, select Aishi's CD110 series with life ratings of 4000-5000 hours at +105°C. The enhanced life performance comes from improved electrolyte formulation and internal construction.

High Ripple Applications

For applications with high ripple current requirements, such as switching power supplies or motor drives, consider Aishi's CD293 series designed specifically for high-frequency, low-impedance operation, or solid polymer capacitors for ultra-low ESR requirements.

High Temperature Applications

For applications operating in high-temperature environments, Aishi's high-temperature series capacitors maintain reliable operation at +125°C, making them ideal for under-hood automotive or industrial applications.

Aishi vs. Competitor Equivalent Selection

Aishi capacitors often provide direct upgrades to competitor parts with improved performance characteristics or cost advantages. When replacing competitor parts:

  • Match rated capacitance and voltage values
  • Check temperature rating and case size
  • Compare ripple current capabilities
  • Consider life rating improvements in Aishi equivalents

Practical Selection Considerations

ESR Impact

Equivalent Series Resistance (ESR) affects both ripple handling capability and heat generation. Lower ESR generally provides better performance but may come at higher cost. For high-frequency applications, solid polymer or low-impedance electrolytic series are recommended.

Case Size Constraints

Aishi offers high-capacitance values in compact case sizes, allowing for miniaturization without sacrificing performance. Consider available board space during selection, but don't compromise on critical electrical parameters for size alone.

Cost vs. Performance

Balancing cost and performance is essential. While using premium capacitors in all locations is unnecessary, using inadequate capacitors in critical positions can cause system failure. For critical applications, use Aishi's long-life or high-reliability series.

  • Life expectancy calculations based on operating conditions
  • Board layout recommendations for optimal thermal performance
  • BOM optimization to reduce costs while maintaining reliability
