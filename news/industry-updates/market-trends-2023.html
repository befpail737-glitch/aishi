2023 Passive Components Market Trends Analysis

September 28, 2023 Industry Updates

The global market for passive electronic components, particularly electrolytic capacitors, continues to show robust growth in 2023, driven primarily by increased demand in renewable energy, automotive electronics, and consumer applications.

Market Drivers

Several key factors are contributing to the sustained growth in the passive components market:

Renewable Energy Sector

The renewable energy sector remains the strongest driver of growth for high-capacity electrolytic capacitors. Solar inverter installations continue to expand globally, with an estimated 340GW of new capacity added in 2023. Wind power installations are also contributing to demand, particularly for high-reliability capacitors capable of withstanding harsh environmental conditions.

Electric Vehicle Market

The electric vehicle (EV) market continues to be a significant growth driver, with increasing adoption of battery electric vehicles (BEVs) and plug-in hybrid electric vehicles (PHEVs). Each EV requires 4-8 times more capacitors than conventional vehicles, particularly for power electronics in battery management systems (BMS), onboard chargers (OBC), and inverter systems.

Consumer Electronics and Computing

Despite some market softness in certain consumer segments, demand for computing and data center applications remains strong. The rollout of new processor architectures and the expansion of cloud computing infrastructure continue to drive demand for high-performance capacitors.

Technology Trends

Several technology trends are shaping the passive components market:

  • Miniaturization: Demand for smaller form factors while maintaining or increasing capacitance values
  • Higher Temperature Operation: Increasing requirement for capacitors rated to 125Â°C and higher
  • Higher Ripple Current: Need for capacitors with improved ripple current handling capabilities
  • AEC-Q200 Compliance: Growing demand for automotive-qualified components

Regional Analysis

Asia-Pacific continues to dominate both production and consumption of passive components, accounting for approximately 70% of global demand. China remains the largest market, followed by Japan and South Korea. Europe and North America represent mature but stable markets with growing demand in automotive and industrial applications.

Supply Chain Considerations

Supply chain stability has improved compared to the extreme shortages of 2021-2022, though some lead time elongation continues in specific product categories. Raw material costs, particularly for aluminum and electrolytes, have stabilized after significant increases in recent years.

Outlook

The market is expected to continue growing at a CAGR of 6-8% through 2027, driven by continued adoption of electrification in automotive, expansion of renewable energy infrastructure, and growth in data center and telecommunications applications.
