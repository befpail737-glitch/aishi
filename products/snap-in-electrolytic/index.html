Snap-in Aluminum Electrolytic Capacitors

High capacitance screw terminal aluminum electrolytic capacitors

About Aishi Snap-in Electrolytic Capacitors

Aishi snap-in aluminum electrolytic capacitors are designed for applications requiring high capacitance values. These capacitors feature screw terminals that allow for easy mounting and connection to the circuit, making them ideal for industrial applications and power supplies with substantial energy storage requirements.

Key Parameters

  • Voltage Range: 16V to 500V
  • Capacitance Range: 470µF to 33,000µF
  • Temperature Range: -40°C to +105°C
  • Mounting: Chassis mounting with threaded terminals
  • Capacitance Tolerance: ±20% (at +20°C, 120Hz)

Typical Applications

Industrial Power Supplies

High-power SMPS and DC-DC converters

Uninterruptible Power Supplies

Energy storage in UPS systems

Motor Drives

Filtering in variable frequency drives

Welding Equipment

Energy storage and filtering

Selection Guide

Snap-in capacitors are ideal when high capacitance values are required and the circuit board cannot accommodate large radial capacitors. Consider factors like available mounting space, vibration requirements, and thermal management when selecting the appropriate series. Read our comprehensive selection guide or contact our FAE team for recommendations.

FAE Application Notes

Our field application engineers recommend snap-in capacitors for high-power applications where substantial energy storage is needed. Ensure proper mounting torque and use star washers to prevent loosening due to vibration.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I properly mount snap-in capacitors?

Use proper mounting hardware with star washers to prevent loosening. Apply recommended torque values to avoid over-tightening which can damage the capacitor.

What's the difference between snap-in and bolt capacitors?

Snap-in capacitors have a base designed to fit into a mounting cup, while bolt capacitors have threaded terminals that mount directly to the chassis.

Can snap-in capacitors be used in high-vibration environments?

Yes, but ensure proper mounting with appropriate hardware to prevent loosening. Consider additional securing methods if necessary.
