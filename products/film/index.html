Film Capacitors

Metallized film capacitors for high reliability applications

About Aishi Film Capacitors

Aishi film capacitors are constructed with a metallized dielectric film and offer excellent stability, low losses, and self-healing properties. These capacitors are ideal for applications requiring high reliability, stability over temperature, and low dielectric absorption.

Key Parameters

  • Voltage Range: 63V to 2000V
  • Capacitance Range: 100pF to 47µF
  • Temperature Range: -55°C to +110°C (depending on dielectric)
  • Dielectric: Polyester (PET), Polypropylene (PP), Polycarbonate (PC)
  • Mounting: Radial, axial, or SMD options

Selection Guide

Choose film capacitors based on voltage requirements, temperature stability needs, and application frequency. For high-frequency applications, polypropylene types offer the lowest losses. For smaller size requirements, polyester types provide higher capacitance per volume. Read our comprehensive selection guide or contact our FAE team for recommendations.

FAE Application Notes

Our field application engineers recommend film capacitors for applications requiring stability over temperature and voltage. Consider dissipation factor (DF) when selecting for high-frequency applications.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does "self-healing" mean?

Self-healing occurs when a dielectric breakdown happens; the metallization around the fault is vaporized, isolating the fault and allowing the capacitor to continue functioning.

What's the difference between polyester and polypropylene film?

Polyester provides higher capacitance per volume but has higher losses. Polypropylene has lower losses and better stability but is physically larger for the same capacitance/voltage.

Can film capacitors be used in AC applications?

Yes, polypropylene film capacitors are especially suitable for AC applications like motor run capacitors and power factor correction.
