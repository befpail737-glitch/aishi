Solid Polymer Capacitors

Ultra low ESR conductive polymer aluminum electrolytic capacitors

About Aishi Solid Polymer Capacitors

Aishi solid polymer aluminum electrolytic capacitors use conductive polymer as the cathode material, providing ultra-low equivalent series resistance (ESR) and high ripple current capability. These capacitors are ideal for high-frequency applications where traditional liquid electrolytic capacitors may not perform adequately.

Key Parameters

  • Voltage Range: 2.5V to 50V
  • Capacitance Range: 10µF to 4700µF
  • Temperature Range: -55°C to +105°C
  • ESR Values: As low as 2mΩ (depending on series)
  • Mounting: SMD or radial lead options

Selection Guide

Solid polymer capacitors are ideal for applications requiring very low ESR and high ripple current capability. Consider voltage derating and thermal management, especially in high-temperature environments. For applications with high inrush current, ensure sufficient capacitance to prevent voltage droop. Read our comprehensive selection guide or contact our FAE team for recommendations.

FAE Application Notes

Our field application engineers recommend solid polymer capacitors for high-frequency DC-DC converters where traditional electrolytics would overheat. These capacitors have excellent stability over temperature and life.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does "solid polymer" mean?

Solid polymer capacitors use a conductive polymer as the cathode material instead of a liquid electrolyte, which results in ultra-low ESR and improved stability.

How do solid polymer capacitors differ from traditional electrolytics?

Solid polymer capacitors have significantly lower ESR, better temperature stability, and longer operational life, but typically have lower capacitance values and higher cost.

Can solid polymer capacitors be used in series or parallel?

Yes, they can be used in series (with balancing resistors) or parallel. Parallel connection is common to increase total capacitance and ripple current capability.
