About Aishi Screw Terminal Electrolytic Capacitors

Aishi screw terminal aluminum electrolytic capacitors are designed for high-power applications requiring both high voltage ratings and high capacitance values. These bolt-type capacitors feature threaded terminals for secure mounting and connection, making them ideal for industrial power equipment and energy storage applications.

Key Parameters

  • Voltage Range: 25V to 550V
  • Capacitance Range: 1000µF to 100,000µF
  • Temperature Range: -25°C to +85°C / +105°C
  • Mounting: Bolt mounting with threaded terminals
  • Capacitance Tolerance: ±20% (at +20°C, 120Hz)

Selection Guide

For high-voltage, high-capacitance applications, Aishi screw terminal capacitors provide reliable performance. Consider voltage derating (typically 10-20%) for long-life applications. For applications with high ripple current, select series with lower equivalent series resistance (ESR). Read our comprehensive selection guide or contact our FAE team for recommendations.

FAE Application Notes

Our field application engineers recommend proper installation with adequate torque to ensure good electrical contact and thermal management. Consider parallel connection for applications requiring very high ripple current handling.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I determine the appropriate torque for mounting?

Follow manufacturer specifications for torque values. Typically ranges from 1.0-3.5 N·m depending on the terminal size. Over-torquing can damage the capacitor.

What's the difference between snap-in and screw terminal capacitors?

Snap-in capacitors mount with a bayonet-style connection to a base, while screw-terminal capacitors have threaded terminals that bolt directly to the mounting surface.

How do I properly connect multiple capacitors in parallel?

Connect positive terminals together and negative terminals together using appropriately sized bus bars. Ensure good thermal contact and equal current sharing.
